IDENTITY - HOPEX Foundation Pattern Map

Description
Top level map of HOPEX patterns. It is layered in three levels::
1. The basic fondations of modeling constructs describing concepts, their relationships and their  classification: the Upper Ontology.
2. The shared characteristics of Architecture Blocks:the Architecture Model Management.
3. The structure and behaviors of systems in their environment: the Architecture GRID Pattern.
The map also includes two dimensions required to ensure enterprise management:
1. Transformation initiatives: the Initiative and Transformation Pattern.
2. Gouvernance activities and instruments: the Governance Pattern.

DOMAINS

DomainDescription
  Architecture GRID Pattern
The  Architecture GRID Pattern domain bundles sub-domains used to model how a system, including an enterprise, operates/functions in its environment.
It includes Agents, Processes, interactions, Behavioral Rules, Capabilityies, Outcomes, Information Objects and Directives which constitute the columns of the EA GRID.
  Architecture Model Management The  Architecture Model Management domain defines top level constructs used in enterprise architecting: Architecture Blocks, Architecture Namespaces, Architecture Category(ies), Constraint Value, Location.
  Governance Pattern The  Governance Pattern domain defines organizations and instrurments required to govern Enduring Initiatives.
  Initiative and Transformation Pattern
The  Initiative and Transformation Pattern domain defines management concepts required for transformation initiatives.
It provides principles for the coordination of large scale initiatives evolving over time (Enduring Initiative and their Initiative Stages).
  Model Block Management
  Upper Ontology
The  Upper Ontology domain encompasses the three fundamental domains that establish the foundation for the modeling syntax..
  • Elementary Graph  defines basic constructs for entities and relationships.
  • Containers & Blocks  defines the concept of reusable entities: building blocks.
  • Aggregate Syntax defines the notion of external and internal structures of building blocks.
