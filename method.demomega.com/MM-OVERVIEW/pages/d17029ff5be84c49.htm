CONCEPT DOMAIN - Product  & Customer Experience

Description Product  & Customer Experience domain describes the overall impression Customers have of the company based on their interactions across multiple Touch Points (CX: customer experience) as well as the perception Customers have of the enterprise's Product based on their engagement with the Products (PX: product experience).

  Business Line
A  Business Line is a high level classification of the main activities of the enterprise. It corresponds for example to major product segments or to distribution channels.
It can be used to classify Business Processes, Organizational Units or Applications that serve a specific product and/or a specific market.
Regulatory Frameworks in some industries impose their own list of  Business Lines.
   Business Line
  Business Outcome
A  Business Outcome is a Business Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Business Object, produced by a Business Operating Agent, for the benefits of an internal or external consumer (especially Customers).
Business Objects range from Information Objects to any organizational constructs (People & Accountability) or Resource Operating Assets offered, produced and delivered by an Enterprise.

  Job-to-be-done
Set of Customer Tasks that contribute to the achievement of the Customer duty(ies).
The granulatity of  Job-to-be-done is determined by outcomes exchanged at Touch Points.
The  Job-to-be-done concept was forged by Clayton Christensen who provided the following definitions in an article written for the Harward Business Review (see references).
1. “Job” is shorthand for what an individual really seeks to accomplish in a given circumstance.
2.. The circumstances are more important than customer characteristics, product attributes, new technologies, or trends.
3. Jobs are never simply about function—they have powerful social and emotional dimensions.

  Value Proposition
A  Value Proposition is a statement that explains the benefits that a Product (goods or services) provides to its Customers. It is essentially a promise made by the enterprise to its target Customers that its Product will fit their need when performing their Job-to-be-done.
A  Value Proposition is a characteristic of a Products (a Product Characteristic) and is defined by:
1) its Functional Value expressed as qualified and quantified Product Capabilitys and their Business Outcomes,
2) its Non Functional Value expressed by Constraint Values.

  Business Operating Capability A  Business Operating Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
This includes Business Capability, Functionality, Hardware Capability and Skill. 		  Enterprise Capability
  Business Partner
A  Business Partner is a party who interacts with the enterprise in the context its operating eco-system (the Business Operating Ecosystem).
Example : Private Sector Customer, Regulation Authority,  Public health Authority.
   Business Partner
  Business System
A  Business System is a man made artifact (Hardware System or Software System) which exposes Functionalityies and can produce Business Outcomes.
A  Business System performs System Processes and participates to System Processes or to Business Processes.
In System Processes, a  Business System is always an active participant (System Process Participant).
In Business Processes, a  Business System is either an active participant (Automated Participant) or an Instrument used by Human Resources.

  Capability Configuration
A  Capability Configuration is a Resource Agent which combines Human Resources and Business Systems along with networking capabilities in order to provide Business Capability(ies).
There are two levels of granularity of  Capability Configurations: System of Systems and System of Resources.

  Constraint Value
A  Constraint Value is a possible value of a Constraining Property such as a delivery time of 30 minutes, a weight of 20 kg, a high level of confidentiality, stormy conditions.
A  Constraint Value is either a single measure (Qualifying Value (Abstract): a weight of 20 kg), an Environmental Value (stormy conditions.) or a Set of Constraint Values.
 Constraint Values are used to constrain Architecture Blocks in the context of non-functional requirement analysis and Value Proposition analysis.
