RELATIONSHIP - Nested Entity

DescriptionAbstract Relationship
A directed relationship that indicates that the source Entity nests its target Entitys.
"Nesting" is also sometimes named "ownership":  the source Entity "owns" the target Entity.

Source Entity  Nesting Entity   Group of Repository Item
Abstract
A  Nesting Entity is an Entity that nests other entities as sub-entities, serving as their lexical scope. The existence, visibility, and significance of Nested Entitys are confined within the boundaries of their  Nesting Entity.
For instance, attributes of a class are nested in their class; attributes exist within their class, they are visible from their class, their meaning is given by their class.
Examples:
- Aggregate Blocks utilize nesting of sub-entities as a mechanism for composability.
-Containers utilize nesting of sub-entities as a mechanism for packaging.
Some modeling standards use the term "Namespace" in place of " Nesting Entity" due to the capability for lexical scoping provided by the latter. However, it is crucial to note that nesting does not always involve naming and namespacing.
