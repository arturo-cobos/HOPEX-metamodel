DEFINITION - Data Lineage

DescriptionData lineage is about tracking the flow of information.
It is necessary to guarantee the quality, usability and security of data.
For large organizations, it is also a key conformity legal requirement: BCBS 239, Solvency II.

Data Lineage is defined as a data life cycle that includes the data’s origins and where it moves over time.
For large organizations, that life cycle can be quite complex as data flows from file, to databases or reports while going through various transformation processes.
Tracking the data provenance of a specific data point is very challenging.
Data Lineage can be presented with nodes and Edges.
