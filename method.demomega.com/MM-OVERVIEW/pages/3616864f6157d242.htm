CONCEPT DOMAIN - Upper Ontology

Description
The  Upper Ontology domain encompasses the three fundamental domains that establish the foundation for the modeling syntax..
  • Elementary Graph  defines basic constructs for entities and relationships.
  • Containers & Blocks  defines the concept of reusable entities: building blocks.
  • Aggregate Syntax defines the notion of external and internal structures of building blocks.

Elementary Graph Pattern Domain
The  Elementary Graph domain defines the top level constructs employed to formalize entities (Entity) and their Relationships. This comprises the formalization of elementary entities (Entity), their elementary relationship (Nested Entity, Referenced Element) and their classification (Class).

Aggregate Syntax Pattern Domain
The purpose of the  Aggregate Syntax is to provide mechanisms to support modular management of complex Building Blocks.
The  Aggregate Syntax handles situations where Entitys have an internal structure. Such Entitys are called Aggregate Entitys. They are made of internal-sub Entitys called Aggregate Entity Members. Aggregate Entity Members can be either strictly local characteristics (called Internal Entity  such as a gateway in a process) or the contextualization of Entitys outside the structure (such as generalization in UML).

Containers & Blocks
The  Containers & Blocks domain defines the core elements of Building Block and Container which provide the basic for modular model management.
Blocks are reusable units. They have an independent existence. Thereby, they cannot be nested in other structures which would hide their existence (lexical scope). Because of their independent existence, they must be owned by an independent artifact which sole purpose is model management: Containers . Containers are dedicated to model management:
1) They own blocks to which they provide existence.
2) They provide namespace to blocks.
3) They may depend on other containers.
