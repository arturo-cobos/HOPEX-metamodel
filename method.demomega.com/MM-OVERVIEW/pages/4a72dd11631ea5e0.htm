OMG - UAF - Function		   Business Process
  Organizational Process
An  Business Process is a set of Operations performed by Human Resources and/or by automated systems (Business Systems) to produce a Business Outcome. It is depicted as a sequence of Operations, controlled by events and conditions.
Operations are carried out by the involvment of Human Resources and system resources (Application) as participants in the process (Participant Resource Agent).
During its course of action, an  Business Process consumes or produces Data Objects.
1) It may read or write Data Objects in its Process Store.
2) It may receive Data Objects at its boundary:  Triggering Business Outcome.
3) It may produce Data Objects at its boundary:  Published Business Outcome.
The course of actions of an  Business Process is constrained by the application of rules (Business Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

 OperationalActivity An Activity that captures a logical process, specified independently of how the process is carried out.
References:
OMG - UAF - OperationalActivity		   Value Stream
  Value Stream
 Value Streams are used to frame the Conceptual Operating Model of the enterprise: they describe how the enterprise operates at the conceptual level and helps chunking responsibilities between Conceptual Agents (Activity Domain or Business Function) .
A  Value Stream is a conceptual Process  performed by Conceptual Agents to produce a Business Outcome. It is depicted as a sequence of Value Stream Activitys, controlled by events and conditions.
Value Stream Activitys are carried out by the involvment of Conceptual Agents as participants in the  Value Stream.
During its course of action, a  Value Stream consumes, produces or stores Business Objects.
1) It may read or write Business Objects in its Business Object Store.
2) It may receive Business Objects at its boundary:  Consumed Business Outcomes.
3) It may produce Business Objects at its boundary:  Delivered Business Outcomes.
The course of actions of a  Value Stream is constrained by the application of rules (Conceptual Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

There are traditionnaly two kinds of  Value Streams:
1) Development development  Value Streams define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required to bring a Product from concept to launch.
2)  Operational  Value Streams define define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required from order to delivery. These include actions to process information from the Customer and actions to transform the product on its way to the Customer.

 ResourceConstraint A rule governing the structural or functional aspects of an implementation.
References:
UAF - ResourceConstraint		   Business Rule
  Operational Rule
An  Business Rule is a rule that is under business jurisdiction.
A rule’s being under "business jurisdiction" means that it is under the jurisdiction of the community that it governs or guides - that the community can opt to change or discard the rule. Laws of physics may be relevant to an Enterprise; legislation and regulations may be imposed on it; external standards. These things are not  Business Rules from the company’s perspective, since it does not have the authority to change them. The company will decide how to react to laws and regulations, and will create  Business Rules to ensure compliance with them. Similarly, it will create  Business Rules to ensure that standards or best practices are implemented as intended.

 OMG - UAF - Rule   Directive
  Directive Building Block
A  Directive is an authoritative declaration that indicates how Agents and their Behaviors should be (or should not be) in the enterprise.
Specifically, a  Directive defines, constrains or liberates some aspects of an Agent and its Behaviors.
 Directives are intended to assert agent structures or to control or influence their Behaviors.  Directives are stated in declarative form.
A  Directive can either be a Policy or a Behavioral Rule.


  Policy
  Policy
A  Policy is a Directive that is not directly enforceable whose purpose is to govern, guide or constrain the structure and behavior of Agents in the enterprise.
Policies provide the basis for rules and govern Behaviors carried out by Agents.
Example of  Policys are Regulation Articles, Business Policys or Architecture principles.
