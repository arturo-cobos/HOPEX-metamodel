  Application
An Application is a Software System that provides a set of Functionality(ies) that End Users see as a single unit.
Essentially  Applications are architectural constructions resulting from the combinaison of the following four criteria:
1) A group of Functionality that End Users see as a single unit.
2) A managed asset ( Managed Application) associated with a budget line in the context of an Application Portfolio.
3) A body of code that is seen by developers as a single unit.
4) A group of deployable software units (Deployable Application Packages) that must be installed together on one or multiple execution nodes (Computational Aspect).
 Application is a Mezzo enterprise asset  that sits between Application System and IT Service in the decomposition of Software Systems.
Example: "Payroll" is an  Application that is part an "HR system" which is an Application System.
The "Payroll"  Application includes, among other things, the "Salary and Wage Calculation" IT Service.

  Business Capability
A  Business Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
A  Business Capability is defined by its intended Business Outcomes and the conditions (Constraining Property) under which the delivery of the Business Outcomes shall be proceeded.
The actual Constraint Values for a given  Business Capability at the different Enterprise Stages is given by their exhibition ( Exhibited Capability).

  Business Outcome
A  Business Outcome is a Business Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Business Object, produced by a Business Operating Agent, for the benefits of an internal or external consumer (especially Customers).
Business Objects range from Information Objects to any organizational constructs (People & Accountability) or Resource Operating Assets offered, produced and delivered by an Enterprise.

  Business Process
An  Business Process is a set of Operations performed by Human Resources and/or by automated systems (Business Systems) to produce a Business Outcome. It is depicted as a sequence of Operations, controlled by events and conditions.
Operations are carried out by the involvment of Human Resources and system resources (Application) as participants in the process (Participant Resource Agent).
During its course of action, an  Business Process consumes or produces Data Objects.
1) It may read or write Data Objects in its Process Store.
2) It may receive Data Objects at its boundary:  Triggering Business Outcome.
3) It may produce Data Objects at its boundary:  Published Business Outcome.
The course of actions of an  Business Process is constrained by the application of rules (Business Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

  Business Rule
An  Business Rule is a rule that is under business jurisdiction.
A rule’s being under "business jurisdiction" means that it is under the jurisdiction of the community that it governs or guides - that the community can opt to change or discard the rule. Laws of physics may be relevant to an Enterprise; legislation and regulations may be imposed on it; external standards. These things are not  Business Rules from the company’s perspective, since it does not have the authority to change them. The company will decide how to react to laws and regulations, and will create  Business Rules to ensure compliance with them. Similarly, it will create  Business Rules to ensure that standards or best practices are implemented as intended.

  Business Scenario
A  Business Scenario is a story describing how Business-Entity (Org-Unit) interact wtiht its partners (Partner Org-Unit) to achieve Business Outcomes in a specific Eco-System (Business Environment).
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Business Object Flows depicting the story towards the delivery of expected Business Outcomes.
2) Interacting  Business Entities (Org-Units)  who participate to the story in the considered Eco-System.
   Scenario of Operational Environment Flows
  Business-Entity Scenario
A  Business-Entity Scenario is a story describing how the components of a Business-Entity (Org-Units) interact to produce Business Outcomes.
This includes:
- A course of events represented by information flows depicting the steps towards the delivery of expected Outcomes.
- Components of an Org-Unit (Sub-Org-Units or Organizational Position) that participate to the story.
   Scenario of Operational Flows

A  Customer is part of an ecosystem and has the ability to choose between different Products.

  Functionality
A  Functionality is a Business Operating Capability offered by Business Systems (software or hardware) and aimed at delivering Information Outcomes.
A  Functionality describes WHAT a software or hardware system can provide.
 Functionality(ies) are used to express the features required by people when performing their job.
For internal customers, these jobs correspond to Operations described in Business Process (see Instrument)
For enterprise Customers, these jobs correspond to Job-to-be-done in the context of Customer Journeys.

  Hardware System
A  Hardware System is a man made tangible artifact which exposes Hardware Capability(ies) and can produce Physical Outcomes.
A  Hardware System performs System Processes and participates to System Processes or to Business Processes.
A  Hardware System can embed Computational Aspects. Together with its embedded Computational Aspects, a  Hardware System can also deliver Information Outcomes.
A  Hardware System may be based on a set of Hardware Technology(ies).
Examples:
- Connected Drone with Online Payment App.
- 3D printer.
- Automated Guided Vehicles (AGVs)
- Connected fridge providing ordering Functionality and of course a freezing Hardware Capability.
- Production equipment in an assembly line (metal forging machine)
- Car

  IT Service
An  IT Service is a functionnal unit of software (java class, COBOL Program, Batch) that is  a consistent, indivisible unit of processing of an Application delivering its Information Outcomes though APIs (Application Interface).
a)  IT Services are assembled and orchestrated in Applications.
b)  IT Services cannot be directly deployed to Computational Aspects: they need to be organized in Deployable Application Packages.
 IT Service is a Micro enterprise asset that sits at the lowest level of Software System decomposition.
Example: the "Salary and Wage Calculation" component is an  IT Service that is part of the "Payroll" Application.

  Value Stream
 Value Streams are used to frame the Conceptual Operating Model of the enterprise: they describe how the enterprise operates at the conceptual level and helps chunking responsibilities between Conceptual Agents (Activity Domain or Business Function) .
A  Value Stream is a conceptual Process  performed by Conceptual Agents to produce a Business Outcome. It is depicted as a sequence of Value Stream Activitys, controlled by events and conditions.
Value Stream Activitys are carried out by the involvment of Conceptual Agents as participants in the  Value Stream.
During its course of action, a  Value Stream consumes, produces or stores Business Objects.
1) It may read or write Business Objects in its Business Object Store.
2) It may receive Business Objects at its boundary:  Consumed Business Outcomes.
3) It may produce Business Objects at its boundary:  Delivered Business Outcomes.
The course of actions of a  Value Stream is constrained by the application of rules (Conceptual Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

There are traditionnaly two kinds of  Value Streams:
1) Development development  Value Streams define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required to bring a Product from concept to launch.
2)  Operational  Value Streams define define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required from order to delivery. These include actions to process information from the Customer and actions to transform the product on its way to the Customer.

  Business System
A  Business System is a man made artifact (Hardware System or Software System) which exposes Functionalityies and can produce Business Outcomes.
A  Business System performs System Processes and participates to System Processes or to Business Processes.
In System Processes, a  Business System is always an active participant (System Process Participant).
In Business Processes, a  Business System is either an active participant (Automated Participant) or an Instrument used by Human Resources.

  Business-Entity Ecosystem
An  Business-Entity Ecosystem is a Resource Ecosystem used to describe the operating context in which a Business-Entity (Org-Unit) exists or lives for a specific purpose.
For instance, the operating context of a company includes its customers and suppliers.
