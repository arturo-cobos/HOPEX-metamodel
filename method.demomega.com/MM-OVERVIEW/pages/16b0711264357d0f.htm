  Assurance Case
An  Assurance Case is a claim that a particular enterprise asset or group of assets (Resources or Activites) adequately mitigates certain identified risks by means of appropriated controls.
An  Assurance Case shall provide confidence that the concerned assets will function as intended in their environment of use.
Privacy Processing Activity(ies), Data Lineages are examples of  Assurance Cases .
    References:
  •  NIST - Assurance Case
  Assurance Instrument
An  Assurance Instrument is a resource or course of actions used by an Assurance System to achieve its objectives.
For instance:
Actions plans are course of actions aimed at solving incidents.
Data Controls are mechanisms used to ensure data quality and data integrity
Privacy Representatives are used to identify national entities in charge on privacy.
  Behavior A  Behavior is an Operating Asset that describes any action or reaction of an Agent to external or internal circumstances. This includes Processes (action), Interaction Scenarios (stories) or interactions (Service Interface).

  Behavioral Rule

Often, a rule is derived from a Policy.  Behavioral Rule are enforced in Processes and Agents.

  Data Category
A  Data Category is a classification or division of Information Objects regarded as having particular shared characteristics.
Examples of  Data Categorys are Health data, Financial data, Contact data.
  Directive
A  Directive is an authoritative declaration that indicates how Agents and their Behaviors should be (or should not be) in the enterprise.
Specifically, a  Directive defines, constrains or liberates some aspects of an Agent and its Behaviors.
 Directives are intended to assert agent structures or to control or influence their Behaviors.  Directives are stated in declarative form.
A  Directive can either be a Policy or a Behavioral Rule.

  Environment Scenario
An  Environment Scenario is a story that describes how an Agent interacts with its partners (Partner Agent) to achieve Outcomes in a specific Operating Eco-System (Agent Environment).
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Object Flows depicting the steps towards the delivery of expected Outcomes.
2) Interacting partners who participate to the story in the considered Operating Eco-System.
  Event
An  Event is type of change that is used to coordinate Behaviors.
An  Event connects a  Predecessor Behavior role producing the event, to a  Successor Behavior role reacting to the event.

  Functional Asset
 Functional Assets encompasse Architecture Blocks used to describe how an enterprise function. This includes the purposes of an enterprise (Capability) and the way the enterprise operates to fulfill these purposes (Agents and their  Behaviors).
 Functional Assets include:
1. Blocks defining results of Behaviors of the enterprise or its sub-systems, that benefit to it internal or external customers : Outcome,
2. Blocks used to describe the capabilities of the enterprise or its sub-systems: Capability,
3. Blocks used to describe information: Information Object.
4. Blocks used to describe how the enterprise operates: Operating Assets (Agent, Process, Interaction Scenario, Service Interface).
All  Functional Assets are constrained by Policys and are exposed to Risk Types.
    References:

  Information Asset
An  Information Asset is a Model Building Block involved in the description of information.
 Information Assets range from Information Objects,  Information Domains to terminologies.
They are defined in Information Dictionary(ies) and managed in Data Catalogs.
  Information Dictionary
An  Information Dictionary is a dictionary of  Information Assets used to describe an information architecture. This includes:
1. Information Domain
2. Information Entity
3. Concept Property
Concrete implementations are Business Dictionary, Logical Data Dictionary, etc.
  Information Domain An  Information Domain is a group of Information Entity(ies) which constitutes a unit a knowlege required by an Agent to operate or required during a Process execution.
  Information Entity
An  Information Entity is an Information Object that is not fundamentally defined by its attributes, but rather by its continuity.
An  Information Entity evolves over time and has states.
For instance a person is an  Information Entity. Employe is a state of a person.

  Information Map
An  Information Map is a top level grouping of Information Domains.
  Information Maps are used to provide navigationtop level entry points for Information Dictionary(ies) and to scope data management initiatives at the level of portfolio management ( Data Catalog), at the project level or at the Enterprise level ( Enterprise Concept Map).
  Information Object
An  Information Object  represents anything that can be communicated or memorized by an Agent in order to produce its Outcomes.
An  Information Object is either an Information Entity or a Information Property.
The difference lies in their relationship to change and to time. Information Entity(ies) can change over time and have a lifeycle while Information Propertys are immutable characteristics.
  Information Property
An  Information Property is an Information Object that represents a characteristic of an Information Entity.
An  Information Property is fundamentally defined by its value. It doesn't evolve over time and is thereby immutable.
For instance "address" or "customer name" are usually considered as  Information Propertys.

  Operating Asset
An  Operating Asset is a Functional Asset used to describes the way Outcomes are produced and consumed: how  (Behaviors) and by whom (Agents).
 Operating Assets include  Agents and their Behaviors (Processes, Service Interfaces and Interaction Scenarios).
 Operating Assets fulfill Capability(ies) (Fulfilled Capability).
As any Functional Asset,  Operating Assets are subject to Policy Conformances. In addition, they define Rule Enforcements to indicate how Policy Conformances are met.
Any  Operating Asset can be considered as a Value Added Object which means it can be transformed and delivered as part of business transactions.
  Operating Context An  Operating Context is a perimeter of Architecture Block (Type) that forms the scope of an Enduring Initiative. It can be a set of Capability(ies) that define the purpose of operating Agents or the Operating Eco-System in which these Agents deliver these Capability(ies).
  Operating Eco-System An  Operating Eco-System is an Operating Context in which an Agent exists or lives for a specific purpose, interacting with partners.
For instance, the operating context of an enterprise includes its customers and suppliers.

  Outcome
An  Outcome is an Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Value Added Object brought about by a specific Behavior,
An  Outcome is a connection point between a producer Behavior and a consumer Behavior which benefits from the produced Value Added Object.
In HOPEX, the most common  Outcome is exemplified by the Information Outcome which refers to the production or consumption of a specific Information Object such as an order received or a  payment rejected.

  Policy
A  Policy is a Directive that is not directly enforceable whose purpose is to govern, guide or constrain the structure and behavior of Agents in the enterprise.
Policies provide the basis for rules and govern Behaviors carried out by Agents.
Example of  Policys are Regulation Articles, Business Policys or Architecture principles.

It ranges from the different kidns of Directives (Business Policy, Architecture principle, Business Rule, etc.),  to classification of policies (Policy Category) up to an entire set of policies (Policy Dictionary).
  Policy Category  Policy Category is a classification of policies. In regulations, it represents sections of the law.

  Process
A  Process is a Behavior that describes a typical course of action intended to produce Outcomes, through the involvement of Agents as Active Participants.
During its course of action, a process consumes or produces Value Added Objects, including Information Objects.
1) It may read or write Information Objects from and to its Process Stores.
2) It may receive Value Added Objects at its boundary:  Consumed Outcomes.
3) It may produce Value Added Objects at its boundary:  Produced Outcomes.
The course of actions of a  Process is constrained (Rule Enforcement) by the application of Behavioral Rules that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

  Regulation Article
Compulsory Policy, define by law, that is not directly enforceable whose purpose is guide enterprise activities.
 Regulation Article are classified by Regulation section  in the context of a Regulatory Framework.
  Regulation section A  Regulation section is a classification of Regulation Articles.
Regulation sections can be themselves organized as a hierarchy.
  Regulatory Framework  Regulatory Framework is a set of Regulation Articles issued by a Regulator (government, international body) to rule the structure and behavior of entities participating to a specific activity domain.

  Risk Type
A  Risk Type refers to a distinct category or classification of risk based on its origin, nature, or potential impact. It helps in organizing and addressing different sources of uncertainty or potential harm that an individual, organization, or system might face. By categorizing risks into different types, entities can develop more targeted mitigation strategies and response plans.
Common risk types include Operational Risk Type, Privacy Risk Type, and Compliance Risk Type, among others.
  Service Interface A  Service Interface is a communication Behavior that describes a typical course of interactions, coordinated by Events and Outcomes, and intended to produce Value Added Objects, through the involvement of Agents.
    References:
  •  OpenGroup - IT4IT - Defining Service Reference Architecture
  Value Added Object
A  Value Added Object is an aspect of Architecture Block (Type)s which can be the subject of business transactions.
A  Value Added Object can be created, enhanced or transformed through the action of a producer Agent, resulting in an increase in value (Outcome) that benefits (value) to a user Agent .
 Value Added Objects include all Operating Assets ( Agents,  Behaviors), Information Objects and Natural Resources.
Examples:
- An improved process (Behavior) that is the result of a refactoring activity done by a consulting organization (producer Agent) for the benefit of a business unit (user Agent).
- A new application (Agent) that is the result of development and sales activities of a software company (producer Agent) for the benefit of an end user (user Agent).
    References:
  •  Russell Ackoff - Choice & Communication - Utility
  •  WordNet - Product (Production)
