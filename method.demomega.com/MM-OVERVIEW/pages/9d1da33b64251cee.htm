DEFINITION CLASSIFIED CONCEPTS

CATEGORY - Business Ontology

Description
Parent Category  EA Concepts   
Classified Concepts
ConceptDescription
  Business Behavior An  Business Behavior is an Business Operating Asset that describes any action or reaction of an Business Operating Agent to external or internal circumstances. This includes Business Operating Processes (actions), Business Operating Scenarios (stories) or service interactions (Business Service Interface).
  Business Capability
A  Business Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
A  Business Capability is defined by its intended Business Outcomes and the conditions (Constraining Property) under which the delivery of the Business Outcomes shall be proceeded.
The actual Constraint Values for a given  Business Capability at the different Enterprise Stages is given by their exhibition ( Exhibited Capability).
    References:
  •  OMG - UAF - Capability
  •  OpenGroup - TOGAF - Definition - Business Capability
  Business Capability Map A  Business Capability Map is a top level assembly of Business Capability(ies) and their dependencies that, together, provide a Business Capability scope for an Enterprise Stage.
  Business Event A  Business Event is a type of business change that is used to coordinate Business Behaviors.
    References:
  •  OpenGroup - TOGAF - Content Metamodel - Event
  Business Object
A  Business Object is an aspect of any tangible or intanglible resource or state of such resource, that can be the subject of a business transaction.
As such, a  Business Object can be improved or transformed in some way by a producer Business Operating Agent, resulting in an increase in value (Business Outcome) that benefits (utility) to a user Business Operating Agent.
 Business Objects include Business Operating Agents themselves, their Behaviors (Business Behavior) as well as Information Objects and Natural Resources.
Examples:
- A new process (Business Behavior) that is the result of a refactoring activity done by a consulting organization (producer Agent) for the benefit of a business unit (user Agent).
- A new application (Business Operating Agent) that is the result of the development and sales activitiies of a software company (producer Agent) for the benefit of an end user (user Agent).
    References:
  •  OMG - BACM - Business Object
  Business Operating Agent
 Business Operating Agent is the supertype of all types of Agents that produce Business Outcomes which benefit to internal or external parties (especially Customers) of the enterprise.
This includes:
1) Conceptual Agents which represent a functional division of labor of the enterprise.
2) Resource Agents which represent  physical resources that produce Business Outcomes of the enterprise.
A  Business Operating Agent is an Agent which is able to participate actively to Business Operating Processes (Participant Operating Agent) and/or to conduct Business Operating Processes (Performed Operating Process) in order to produce Business Outcomes.
  Business Operating Asset
 Business Operating Assets comprise all resources which contributes to the delivery of Business Outcomes of the enterprise.
 Business Operating Assets are subject to Operational Risks.
  Business Operating Capability A  Business Operating Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
This includes Business Capability, Functionality, Hardware Capability and Skill.
  Business Operating Context An  Business Operating Context is a perimeter of Architecture Block (Type) that forms the functional scope of an Enterprise. It can be a set of Business Operating Capability(ies) that define the purpose of Business Operating Agents or the Business Operating Ecosystem in which these Business Operating Agents deliver these Business Operating Capability(ies).
  Business Operating Ecosystem
An  Business Operating Ecosystem is an operating context in which an Business Operating Agent exists or lives for a specific purpose.
For instance, the operating context of an enterprise includes its Business Partners (customers and suppliers).
  Business Operating Environment A  Business Operating Environment is an operating context which defines the interactions ( Business Service Channel) of an Business Operating Agent with its partners (Partner Operating Agent).
    References:
  •  Russell Ackoff - System of Concepts - Environment of a system
  Business Operating Environment Scenario
A  Business Operating Environment Scenario is a story that describes how an Business Operating Agent interacts with its partners (Partner Operating Agent) to achieve Business Outcomes in a specific Operating Eco-System (Business Operating Environment).
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Business Object Flows depicting the story towards the delivery of expected Business Outcomes.
2) Interacting partners who participate to the story in the considered Business Operating Ecosystem.
  Business Operating Process
A  Business Operating Process is a Process that describes a typical course of action intended to produce Business Outcomes, through the involvement of Business Operating Agents (Participant Operating Agent).
During its course of action, a  Business Operating Process consumes or produces Business Objects:
1) It may produce or consume Business Objects from and to its Process Stores.
2) It may receive Business Objects at its boundary:  Triggering Business Outcomes.
3) It may produce Business Objects at its boundary:  Published Business Outcomes.
The course of actions of an  Business Operating Process is constrained by the application of rules (Operating Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.
Within HOPEX, we can examine  Business Operating Processes from three distinct perspectives:
a) A conceptual standpoint is provided by Value Streams.
b) An operational business perspective is offered by Business Processes.
c) An automated viewpoint is provided by System Processes.
  Business Operating Rule An  Business Operating Rule is a Behavioral Rule that is enforced by Business Operating Assets.
  Business Operating Scenario
A  Business Operating Scenario is a story that describes how the components of an Business Operating Agent interacts to produce Business Outcomes.
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Business Object Flows depicting the steps towards the delivery of expected Business Outcomes.
2) Interacting Business Operating Agents who participate to the story.
  Business Outcome
A  Business Outcome is a Business Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Business Object, produced by a Business Operating Agent, for the benefits of an internal or external consumer (especially Customers).
Business Objects range from Information Objects to any organizational constructs (People & Accountability) or Resource Operating Assets offered, produced and delivered by an Enterprise.
    References:
  •  OMG - BACM - Outcome
  •  OMG - UAF - Effect
  •  OMG - UAF - OperationalSignal
  •  OpenGroup - IT4IT - Defining Service Reference Architecture
  •  OpenGroup - OAA - Definition - Outcome
  Business Partner
A  Business Partner is a party who interacts with the enterprise in the context its operating eco-system (the Business Operating Ecosystem).
Example : Private Sector Customer, Regulation Authority,  Public health Authority.
  Business Service Interface A  Business Service Interface is a communication behavior that describes a typical course of interactions intended to produce Business Outcomes, through the involvement of Business Operating Agents.
    References:
  •  OMG - UAF - Operational Interface
  •  OpenGroup - IT4IT - Defining Service Reference Architecture

  Operational Risk Type
An  Operational Risk Type is a kind of Risk Type that refers to the potential for loss due to the enterprise's inadequate functioning.
An  Operational Risk Type is either Compliance Risk Type a or an Operating Risk Type.
  Skill A  Skill is an ability of a human resource that has been acquired by training.
    References:
  •  OMG - UAF - Competence
  •  Wordnet - Skill
  Skill Map A  Skill Map is Capability Map an assembly of Skills and their dependencies that, together, defines the scope of a Human Resource Architecture.
