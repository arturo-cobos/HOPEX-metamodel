BUILDING BLOCK - Business Operating Process

DescriptionAbstract Concept
A  Business Operating Process is a Process that describes a typical course of action intended to produce Business Outcomes, through the involvement of Business Operating Agents (Participant Operating Agent).
During its course of action, a  Business Operating Process consumes or produces Business Objects:
1) It may produce or consume Business Objects from and to its Process Stores.
2) It may receive Business Objects at its boundary:  Triggering Business Outcomes.
3) It may produce Business Objects at its boundary:  Published Business Outcomes.
The course of actions of an  Business Operating Process is constrained by the application of rules (Operating Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.
Within HOPEX, we can examine  Business Operating Processes from three distinct perspectives:
a) A conceptual standpoint is provided by Value Streams.
b) An operational business perspective is offered by Business Processes.
c) An automated viewpoint is provided by System Processes.
