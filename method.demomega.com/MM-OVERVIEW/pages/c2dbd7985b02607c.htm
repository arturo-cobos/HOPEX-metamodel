ConceptDescriptionHOPEX Metaclass
  Business Capability
A  Business Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
A  Business Capability is defined by its intended Business Outcomes and the conditions (Constraining Property) under which the delivery of the Business Outcomes shall be proceeded.
The actual Constraint Values for a given  Business Capability at the different Enterprise Stages is given by their exhibition ( Exhibited Capability).

  Business Outcome
A  Business Outcome is a Business Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Business Object, produced by a Business Operating Agent, for the benefits of an internal or external consumer (especially Customers).
Business Objects range from Information Objects to any organizational constructs (People & Accountability) or Resource Operating Assets offered, produced and delivered by an Enterprise.

  Functionality
A  Functionality is a Business Operating Capability offered by Business Systems (software or hardware) and aimed at delivering Information Outcomes.
A  Functionality describes WHAT a software or hardware system can provide.
 Functionality(ies) are used to express the features required by people when performing their job.
For internal customers, these jobs correspond to Operations described in Business Process (see Instrument)
For enterprise Customers, these jobs correspond to Job-to-be-done in the context of Customer Journeys.
    References:

  Hardware Capability
A  Hardware Capability is a Business Operating Capability offered by Hardware Systems and aimed at delivering Physical Outcomes.
A Hardware System describes WHAT a Hardware System can provide.
 Hardware Capability(ies) are used to express the features required by people when performing their job.
For internal customers, these jobs correspond to  Operations described in Business Processes (see Instrument).
For enterprise Customers, these jobs correspond to Job-to-be-done in the context of Customer Journeys.
Examples:
- 3D Printing.
-  Automated Parcel Delivery.
- Metal Shaping.

  Information Outcome
An  Information Outcome is a Business Outcome that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of Data Objects produced by the enterprise or by one of its sub-system, for the benefits of an internal or external party.
Example:
- Order received
- Payment rejected

  Software Technology Capability
A  Software Technology Capability is a Capability offered by Software Technology Systems and aimed at delivering Software Technology Outcomes.
 Software Technology Capability(ies) define WHAT Software Technology Systems can do, They are used to understand whether two types of Software Technology Systems are fundamentally doing the same thing.
They are also used in Software Technology ArcDevOps to identify where there are redundancies in Technology Portfolios.

  Conceptual Object
A  Conceptual Object is the representation of any tangible or intanglible resource, or its respective state, that is critical for comprehending an enterprise, including its data, resources, and activities.
Similar to any Information Object, a  Conceptual Object can be classified into three categories:
1)  Conceptual Entitys denote entities that can change over time.
2) Event Concepts embody the temporal boundaries associated with Conceptual Entitys.
3) Concept Propertys represent immutable characteristics of Conceptual Entitys.

  Constraining Property
A  Constraining Property expresses a type of constraint that can be applied to Architecture Blocks.
These constraints can be either Measurable Property (Abstract) (Time, Mass, Cost, ..), Environmental Propertys (Terrain Type, Light conditions, Weather conditions) or a grouping of these (Set of Constraining Properties).
 Constraining Propertys are used to constrain Architecture Blocks in the context of non-functional requirement analysis and Value Proposition analysis.

  Logical Data Object
A  Logical Data Object represents the logical structure of any kind of data that can be memorized by a Software System.
A  Logical Data Object is either a Logical Data Entity or a Logical Data Property.
Only Logical Data Entitys can have identity and can be referenced by Logical Relationships. Logical Data Propertys only handle raw data.
