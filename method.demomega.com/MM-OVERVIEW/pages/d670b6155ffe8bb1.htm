CONCEPT DOMAIN - EA Pattern - Business Resource Operating Pattern

DescriptionPattern Domain
The  EA Pattern - Business Resource Operating Pattern domain defines concepts and relationships used to model how physical resources (Resource Operating Assets) operates/functions in Resource Ecosystems to deliver Business Outcomes.
Resource Operating Asset is an abstraction for Human Resource, Software System, Hardware System and Network connectivity and their behaviors (Resource Process, Resource Interaction Scenario).
The  EA Pattern - Business Resource Operating Pattern is used as a template for all concrete operating domains such as People & Accountability or Application Functional Architecture.

  Business Capability
A  Business Capability is a Capability that benefits to Customers (internal or external) of the enterprise. It expresses an ability to deliver Business Outcomes.
A  Business Capability is defined by its intended Business Outcomes and the conditions (Constraining Property) under which the delivery of the Business Outcomes shall be proceeded.
The actual Constraint Values for a given  Business Capability at the different Enterprise Stages is given by their exhibition ( Exhibited Capability).

  Business Outcome
A  Business Outcome is a Business Event that indicates the availability of an expected change in the state of a Business Object, produced by a Business Operating Agent, for the benefits of an internal or external consumer (especially Customers).
Business Objects range from Information Objects to any organizational constructs (People & Accountability) or Resource Operating Assets offered, produced and delivered by an Enterprise.

  Value Stream
 Value Streams are used to frame the Conceptual Operating Model of the enterprise: they describe how the enterprise operates at the conceptual level and helps chunking responsibilities between Conceptual Agents (Activity Domain or Business Function) .
A  Value Stream is a conceptual Process  performed by Conceptual Agents to produce a Business Outcome. It is depicted as a sequence of Value Stream Activitys, controlled by events and conditions.
Value Stream Activitys are carried out by the involvment of Conceptual Agents as participants in the  Value Stream.
During its course of action, a  Value Stream consumes, produces or stores Business Objects.
1) It may read or write Business Objects in its Business Object Store.
2) It may receive Business Objects at its boundary:  Consumed Business Outcomes.
3) It may produce Business Objects at its boundary:  Delivered Business Outcomes.
The course of actions of a  Value Stream is constrained by the application of rules (Conceptual Rule Enforcement) that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

There are traditionnaly two kinds of  Value Streams:
1) Development development  Value Streams define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required to bring a Product from concept to launch.
2)  Operational  Value Streams define define all of the actions, both value-creating and nonvalue-creating, required from order to delivery. These include actions to process information from the Customer and actions to transform the product on its way to the Customer.

  Business Object
A  Business Object is an aspect of any tangible or intanglible resource or state of such resource, that can be the subject of a business transaction.
As such, a  Business Object can be improved or transformed in some way by a producer Business Operating Agent, resulting in an increase in value (Business Outcome) that benefits (utility) to a user Business Operating Agent.
 Business Objects include Business Operating Agents themselves, their Behaviors (Business Behavior) as well as Information Objects and Natural Resources.
Examples:
- A new process (Business Behavior) that is the result of a refactoring activity done by a consulting organization (producer Agent) for the benefit of a business unit (user Agent).
- A new application (Business Operating Agent) that is the result of the development and sales activitiies of a software company (producer Agent) for the benefit of an end user (user Agent).
    References:
  •  OMG - BACM - Business Object
  Conceptual Agent
A  Conceptual Agent is a type of Business Operating Agent that depicts a functional division of labor within an enterprise, influencing the formation of its business operating model.
The concrete realizations of  Conceptual Agent come in the form of Activity Domain (a macro  Conceptual Agent) and Business Function (a mezzo  Conceptual Agent).
    References:

  Conceptual Operating Asset
A  Conceptual Operating Asset is an Operating Asset  used to describe the Conceptual Operating Model of the enterprise.
It includes Value Streams, Activity Domains and Business Functions and the way they contribute to the delivery of Business Outcomes.
    References:

  Data Domain
A  Data Domain is subset of the enterprise’s data that are meant to be manipulated together in the context of business operations.
For instance, a "Sales"  Data Domain contains at least the following entities: Customers, Orders, Products.
Each Data Entity in a  Data Domain has CRUD characteristics. For instance, Products are read-only in a "Sales"  Data Domain, while Customers and Orders have all CRUD characteristics.
 Data Domains define functional data boundaries used both for Data Allocation to Business Systems (see Data Stores) and Data Governance for data stewardship (Data Catalogs).

  Resource Agent
A  Resource Agent is a Business Operating Agent which represents physical resources that produce Business Outcomes of the enterprise.
A  Resource Agent can be a Human Resource (Organizational Position or Org-Unit), a Business System (Software System or Hardware System) or a Capability Configuration (an assembly of Human Resources and Business Systems.)

  Resource Agent Environment A  Resource Agent Environment is an operating context which defines the interactions ( Business Service Channel) of a Resource Agent with its partners (Partner Resource Agent).
  Resource Asset (Individual)
  Resource Behavior A  Resource Behavior is a Resource Operating Asset that describes any action or reaction of a Resource Agent to external or internal circumstances. This includes Resource Processes (actions), Resource Interaction Scenarios (stories) or service interactions (Business Service Interface).
  Resource Ecosystem A  Resource Ecosystem is an Operating Eco-System  that describes the operating context in which a Resource Agent exists or lives for a specific purpose.
  Resource Environment Scenario
A  Resource Environment Scenario is a story that describes how a Resource Agent interacts with its partners (Partner Resource Agents) to achieve Business Outcomes in a specific Eco-System (Resource Agent Environment).
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Business Object Flows depicting the story towards the delivery of expected Business Outcomes.
2) Interacting  Resource Agents who participate to the story in the considered Eco-System.
  Resource Interaction Scenario
A  Resource Interaction Scenario is a story involving the components of a Resource Agent interacting in order to produce Business Outcomes.
This includes:
1) A course of events represented by  Resource Flows depicting the steps towards the delivery of expected Business Outcomes.
2) Component Resource Agents that participate to the story.

A  Resource Process is a Business Operating Process which is conducted by Resource Agents (physical resources such as Human Resource, System of Resources ,Business Systems) and which describes how these entities act to deliver Business Outcomes.
 Resource Processes include Business Processes and System Processes.
