BUILDING BLOCK - Business Object

DescriptionAbstract Concept (Aspect)
A  Business Object is an aspect of any tangible or intanglible resource or state of such resource, that can be the subject of a business transaction.
As such, a  Business Object can be improved or transformed in some way by a producer Business Operating Agent, resulting in an increase in value (Business Outcome) that benefits (utility) to a user Business Operating Agent.
 Business Objects include Business Operating Agents themselves, their Behaviors (Business Behavior) as well as Information Objects and Natural Resources.
Examples:
- A new process (Business Behavior) that is the result of a refactoring activity done by a consulting organization (producer Agent) for the benefit of a business unit (user Agent).
- A new application (Business Operating Agent) that is the result of the development and sales activitiies of a software company (producer Agent) for the benefit of an end user (user Agent).
