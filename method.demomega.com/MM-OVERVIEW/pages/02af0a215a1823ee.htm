DOMAINS

NameDescription
Aggregate Syntax
The purpose of the  Aggregate Syntax is to provide mechanisms to support modular management of complex Building Blocks.
The  Aggregate Syntax handles situations where Entitys have an internal structure. Such Entitys are called Aggregate Entitys. They are made of internal-sub Entitys called Aggregate Entity Members. Aggregate Entity Members can be either strictly local characteristics (called Internal Entity  such as a gateway in a process) or the contextualization of Entitys outside the structure (such as generalization in UML).
Appraisal PatternThe  Appraisal Pattern domain defines constructs used by stakeholders to asses and qualify Architecture Blocks.
Architecture GRID Pattern
The  Architecture GRID Pattern domain bundles sub-domains used to model how a system, including an enterprise, operates/functions in its environment.
It includes Agents, Processes, interactions, Behavioral Rules, Capabilityies, Outcomes, Information Objects and Directives which constitute the columns of the EA GRID.
Architecture Model ManagementThe  Architecture Model Management domain defines top level constructs used in enterprise architecting: Architecture Blocks, Architecture Namespaces, Architecture Category(ies), Constraint Value, Location.
Containers & Blocks
The  Containers & Blocks domain defines the core elements of Building Block and Container which provide the basic for modular model management.
Blocks are reusable units. They have an independent existence. Thereby, they cannot be nested in other structures which would hide their existence (lexical scope). Because of their independent existence, they must be owned by an independent artifact which sole purpose is model management: Containers . Containers are dedicated to model management:
1) They own blocks to which they provide existence.
2) They provide namespace to blocks.
3) They may depend on other containers.
Elementary GraphThe  Elementary Graph domain defines the top level constructs employed to formalize entities (Entity) and their Relationships. This comprises the formalization of elementary entities (Entity), their elementary relationship (Nested Entity, Referenced Element) and their classification (Class).
Governance PatternThe  Governance Pattern domain defines organizations and instrurments required to govern Enduring Initiatives.
Indicator Pattern
Information PatternThe  Information Pattern domain describes top level concepts used to describe information.
Initiative and Transformation Pattern
The  Initiative and Transformation Pattern domain defines management concepts required for transformation initiatives.
It provides principles for the coordination of large scale initiatives evolving over time (Enduring Initiative and their Initiative Stages).
Legend
Model Block Management
Operating SemanticThe  Operating Semantic domain is used to model how a system (Agent) operates/functions (Behavior) in its Operating Eco-System to produce Outcomes that benefit (value) to other Agents or affect (have an effect upon) these Agents.
It includes Agents, Processes, Interaction Scenarios, Policy(ies), Capability(ies), Outcomes.
Policy PatternThe  Policy Pattern domain defines Directives and rules that constrain enterprise structures and behaviors.
Resolution PatternThe  Resolution Pattern domain defines constructs used by stakeholders to assess and qualify Functional Assets.
Risk & Threat
Teams Pattern
The  Teams Pattern domain defines organization structure and behavior as well as instrurments required to govern Enduring Initiatives.
Any Initiative, (project, enterprise, asset portfolio, privacy insurance, etc.) shall be governed. This includes:
1. Governance Committees in charge of governance. They comprise persons (person (system) in HOPEX) and the role of these individual in the team (architects, auditors, product owner, etc.).
2. Governance Events describe changes that need to be recorded to monitor transformations (décisions, incidents, etc.).
3. Governance Activity(ies) describe all actions that come in response to change events and may results in other change events (workflow, audits, ...).
4. Measures provide KPIs (qualification and quantification) on the status of  Undertakings being governed.
Upper Ontology
The  Upper Ontology domain encompasses the three fundamental domains that establish the foundation for the modeling syntax..
  • Elementary Graph  defines basic constructs for entities and relationships.
  • Containers & Blocks  defines the concept of reusable entities: building blocks.
  • Aggregate Syntax defines the notion of external and internal structures of building blocks.

  Idea
An Idea is a potential modification which could be carried out to the architectural landscape in order to improve its capabilities.
Its impact and scope can be defined through Idea Subjects; Idea subjects may concern capabilities and / or items of the architectural landscape.
Ideas may also be freely defined and not related to an identified architectural artifact within the architecture landscape (e.g. disruptive ideas do not necessarily relate to a Solution Building Block or Capability).
Ideas can be gathered as part of an Ideation Campaign (top / down approach) or submitted at any time (bottom-up approach). In both cases Ideas will feed the ideation funnel.
The Ideas are evaluated by Innovation Managers; they can give birth to formal projects requests (demands) as part of the ideation / project funnel and further evaluated by the projects demand management process.

  Class
A  Class is collection of Entitys which it classifies according to one or more criteria.
In knowledge representation, a  Class can be defined either by extension (specifying members), or by intension (specifying criteria), using what is called in some ontology languages like OWL.
Not two  Classes have the same membership. However, a distinction must be made between a  Class having members, and those members being known, so within an information system the members recorded may change over time, even though the true membership does not change.
Note: The HOPEX Foundation is a higher-order ontology, so  Classes may have members that are also  Classes (there are  Classes of  Classes).

  Encapsulated Aggregate
An  Encapsulated Aggregate is a Aggregate Block where its internal structure and state changes are hidden from the outside world by means of a formal boundary.
The only way to access to an  Encapsulated Aggregate is through this formal boundary, which ensures the aggregate's internal consistency.
Within an  Encapsulated Aggregate structure, Contextual Objects can be linked together through  Block Connections.
Examples:
- Processes, their sequenced operations and their boundary events.
- Applications, their connected IT-services and their APIs boundaries (Service Points and Request Points).

  Enduring Initiative
An  Enduring Initiative is a long term Initiative which involves roadmapping and governance. Enterprises and their Management Systems are typical  Enduring Initiatives.
1.  Enduring Initiatives are purpose-oriented.
2.  Enduring Initiatives are change management systems: they have objectives regarding the purposes they pursue, maintain and develop.
3.  Enduring Initiatives are measured to ensure they meet their Goals.
4.  Enduring Initiatives are organized according to subsidiary levels, ensuring autonomy and ownership at each level of delegation while providing responsibility and accountability in achieving common goals.
5.  Enduring Initiatives are under the control of an associed Governing Team.
6.Governing Teams have instruments used to carry out governance: responsibility assignment, assessments, workflow, decision records, incident records.

  Functional Asset
 Functional Assets encompasse Architecture Blocks used to describe how an enterprise function. This includes the purposes of an enterprise (Capability) and the way the enterprise operates to fulfill these purposes (Agents and their  Behaviors).
 Functional Assets include:
1. Blocks defining results of Behaviors of the enterprise or its sub-systems, that benefit to it internal or external customers : Outcome,
2. Blocks used to describe the capabilities of the enterprise or its sub-systems: Capability,
3. Blocks used to describe information: Information Object.
4. Blocks used to describe how the enterprise operates: Operating Assets (Agent, Process, Interaction Scenario, Service Interface).
All  Functional Assets are constrained by Policys and are exposed to Risk Types.

N
  Nesting Entity
A  Nesting Entity is an Entity that nests other entities as sub-entities, serving as their lexical scope. The existence, visibility, and significance of Nested Entitys are confined within the boundaries of their  Nesting Entity.
For instance, attributes of a class are nested in their class; attributes exist within their class, they are visible from their class, their meaning is given by their class.
Examples:
- Aggregate Blocks utilize nesting of sub-entities as a mechanism for composability.
-Containers utilize nesting of sub-entities as a mechanism for packaging.
Some modeling standards use the term "Namespace" in place of " Nesting Entity" due to the capability for lexical scoping provided by the latter. However, it is crucial to note that nesting does not always involve naming and namespacing.

  Process
A  Process is a Behavior that describes a typical course of action intended to produce Outcomes, through the involvement of Agents as Active Participants.
During its course of action, a process consumes or produces Value Added Objects, including Information Objects.
1) It may read or write Information Objects from and to its Process Stores.
2) It may receive Value Added Objects at its boundary:  Consumed Outcomes.
3) It may produce Value Added Objects at its boundary:  Produced Outcomes.
The course of actions of a  Process is constrained (Rule Enforcement) by the application of Behavioral Rules that define what is allowed and not allowed to do.

  Value Added Object
A  Value Added Object is an aspect of Architecture Block (Type)s which can be the subject of business transactions.
A  Value Added Object can be created, enhanced or transformed through the action of a producer Agent, resulting in an increase in value (Outcome) that benefits (value) to a user Agent .

- An improved process (Behavior) that is the result of a refactoring activity done by a consulting organization (producer Agent) for the benefit of a business unit (user Agent).
