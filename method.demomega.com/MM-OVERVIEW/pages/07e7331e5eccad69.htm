CONCEPT DOMAIN - HOPEX - IRM

DescriptionGet an integrated view of risks, controls, incidents, and compliance with business operations, so that risk is treated as a whole and not in silos. Identify risks, implement related controls, declare incidents in a structured library and map regulatory requirements to processes and IT assets.

ConceptDescriptionHOPEX Metaclass
  Control A control is a set of rules and means enabling assurance that a legal, regulatory, internal or strategic requirement is respected.

  Control Directive A  Control Directive is a kind of Policy that provides recommendations on how to comply with Regulation Articles.
Once implemented,  Control Directives enforces any Regulatory Framework your organization has to comply with.
    References:
  •  GRCschema.org - Control

  Control System A control system is a set of controls that ensure risk prevention and management, application of internal operating rules, respect a law or regulation, or work towards achievement of an objective as defined by company strategy. Examples: quality control system, control system relating to IT and Privacy, management control system, internal audit system.   Control System
  Control Type Control types allow classification of controls implemented in a company in accordance with regulatory or domain specific standards (Cobit, etc.).   Control Type
  Operational Incident The incident impacted elements are a subpart of the risk’s scope. The incident causes and consequences are a subpart of the risk’s ones. But the incident scope (processes, entities etc.) are not filtered within the scope of the risk connected to the incident, because the link between risk and incident isn’t always done.   Incident
  Process Family A   Process Family is a category of Process.   Business Process
  Regulatory Framework  Regulatory Framework is a set of Regulation Articles issued by a Regulator (government, international body) to rule the structure and behavior of entities participating to a specific activity domain.
    References:

 		  Regulatory Framework
  Risk Assessment An assessment session is an assessment carried out over a determined time period. When an assessment session is published, an assessment questionnaire containing questions is sent to targeted assessors.   Assessment Node
  Risk Factor A risk factor is an element which contributes to the occurrence of a risk or which triggers a risk. Several risks can originate from a same risk factor. Examples: the use of a hazardous chemical product, the complexity of an application, the size of a project, the number of involved parties, the use of a new technology, the lack of quality insurance, the lack of rigor in requirements definition, etc.   Risk Factor

  Operational Risk Type
An  Operational Risk Type is a kind of Risk Type that refers to the potential for loss due to the enterprise's inadequate functioning.
An  Operational Risk Type is either Compliance Risk Type a or an Operating Risk Type.
